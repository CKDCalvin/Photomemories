Development Defense

This page documents the intent, process, and rationale behind the final website. It explains how milestone planning shaped the final design and how the site serves the target audience of local photography clients.

The website was designed to help visitors move from awareness to confidence to booking. Each page supports that path through organized hierarchy, consistent branding, and accessible interaction.

Design intent

The business planning document established the site focus: showcase photography services, build trust, and encourage booking. The final site keeps that focus with a home page hero, visible calls to action, client reviews, and service previews.

Professional presentation

The wheat, seaweed, and neutral palette came directly from the aesthetics milestone. These colors keep the site clean and calm while letting images remain the visual priority.

Ease of use

The navigation remains consistent on every page, forms use labels and helper text, and the pricing table reduces confusion before booking. Mobile navigation is included for smaller screens.

Page-by-page rationale

Home

The home page introduces the business and quickly answers three questions: what the business offers, why it is trustworthy, and what the visitor should do next.

Milestone connection: the home page acts as the hub in the hierarchical structure chosen earlier in the course.

Services and Pricing

Service cards break content into understandable categories, while the pricing table supports side-by-side comparison. This improves readability and helps visitors self-select the right package.

Contact / Booking

The contact page combines inquiry and booking because in the milestone booking was the main desired user action. Dropdowns and datalist suggestions reduce typing effort for the user.

Portfolio / About / Summary

Together, they support the client's confidence before and after booking.

Opportunities for improvement and growth

If more time and resources were available, future updates could include implementation of React, a searchable gallery, a client and admin portal with a MongoDB for data storage, online payment integration, real scheduling availability synced to a calendar. Additional content could include blog posts with session tips, seasonal promotions, and a FAQ page for outfit planning, turnaround times, and print options.
